PowerLog Documentation

Complete guide to every feature in the all-in-one powerlifting companion app.

Overview

PowerLog is a full-stack powerlifting companion designed for lifters of all levels — from beginners tracking their first session to advanced competitors preparing for meets. The app combines essential lifting tools with intelligent personalization features to help you train smarter.

Built with Go + Fiber + GORM (backend), TypeScript + React + Zustand (frontend), and MariaDB (database), PowerLog runs entirely in Docker containers for easy deployment.

Onboarding Wizard

New Personalization

When a new user registers and logs in for the first time, they are guided through a 5-step onboarding wizard before accessing the main app. This ensures the app is tailored to their profile from day one.

The 5 Steps

  1. Welcome — Brief introduction and goal selection (lose weight, build muscle, increase strength, competition prep, general fitness).
  2. Experience & Training — Select experience level (beginner / intermediate / advanced) and training frequency (2-6 days/week).
  3. Equipment & Focus — Choose available equipment (barbell, dumbbell, squat rack, cables, machines) and training focus areas.
  4. Goals & Preferences — Set initial SBD 1RM targets and training style preference (Push/Pull/Legs, Upper/Lower, Full Body, Custom).
  5. Complete — Summary of all selections with a "Start Training" button to enter the app.

After completing onboarding, the user is redirected to the Dashboard. Onboarding data is stored in user_profiles and startup_preferences tables.

Tip: Users can revisit and update their preferences later from the profile/settings area. The onboarding status is tracked so it only shows once.

Authentication

PowerLog supports multiple authentication methods:

  • Email & Password — Standard registration and login with JWT tokens (default 72h expiry).
  • Google OAuth2 — Sign in with your Google account.
  • Facebook OAuth2 — Sign in with Facebook.
  • Twitter OAuth2 — Sign in with Twitter/X.

OAuth providers are configured via environment variables. The frontend handles the OAuth callback flow and stores the JWT token in Zustand state.

Dashboard

The Dashboard is the first screen users see after login. It provides a snapshot of key metrics and can be customized with widgets.

Default Widgets

  • Weekly Volume Chart — Total volume (weight × reps) over the last 7 days for SBD lifts.
  • Latest PRs — Recent personal records for squat, bench, and deadlift.
  • Recovery Score — Current recovery status based on recent sleep, stress, and DOMS logs.
  • Daily Checklist — Quick overview of today's warmup/cooldown completion status.
  • Goal Progress — Progress bars for active goals.
  • Recommendations — Latest rule-based and AI recommendations.

Widgets can be toggled on/off via the "Atur Widget" button on the Dashboard.

1RM Calculator

Calculate your estimated one-rep max using three validated formulas:

  • Epley — weight × (1 + reps / 30) — Most common, good for all rep ranges.
  • Brzycki — weight × (36 / (37 - reps)) — Best accuracy for low reps (1-10).
  • Lombardi — weight × reps^0.10 — Tends to be more conservative.

Enter weight, reps, and select a formula to instantly see your estimated 1RM. Results include the weight, percentage of 1RM, and recommended training zones.

Lift Tracking

Log every squat, bench press, and deadlift session with detailed data:

  • Lift Type — Squat, Bench Press, or Deadlift.
  • Weight & Reps — Sets with weight in kg and number of reps.
  • RPE — Rate of Perceived Exertion (1-10) to gauge intensity.
  • Notes — Optional notes about the session.

All records are stored with timestamps and never deleted (soft delete via GORM). The lift history feeds into the Analytics, Recommendations, and Goal Progress features.

Exercise Library

Discover accessory exercises tailored to your main lifts and experience level. Each exercise includes:

  • Target muscle group and lift association (squat/bench/deadlift).
  • Difficulty rating and recommended sets/reps.
  • Exercise description and execution tips.

Accessories are organized by which main lift they support, helping you build balanced accessory work around your SBD training.

Warmup & Cooldown Checklist

Follow structured warmup and cooldown routines with an interactive checklist:

  • Warmup — Dynamic stretches, activation exercises, and ramp-up sets.
  • Cooldown — Static stretches and mobility work to aid recovery.

Each item can be checked off as completed. The checklist resets daily, and completion status is tracked in the Dashboard.

Body Weight Tracking

Log your daily body weight to track changes over time. Features include:

  • Simple weight input in kg.
  • History view with a weight trend chart.
  • Visual progress over weeks and months.

Body weight data is used by the Recommendation Engine to provide context-aware suggestions.

Recovery Logging

New Personalization

Track your daily recovery status across three key metrics:

  • Sleep Hours — Total hours slept (0-24).
  • Sleep Quality — Self-rated quality on a scale of 1-10.
  • Stress Level — Daily stress rating (1-10).
  • DOMS Level — Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness rating (1-10).

Each log produces a Recovery Score (0-100) calculated from the weighted combination of these metrics. Recovery trends over time are displayed in the Analytics page and used by the Recommendation Engine to detect when you need to deload.

Formula: Recovery Score = (Sleep Hours / 8 × 30) + (Sleep Quality × 2) + ((11 - Stress) × 2) + ((11 - DOMS) × 2). Max score is 100.

Analytics

Pro

Comprehensive analytics to visualize your training data:

  • Weekly Volume Chart — Bar chart showing total SBD volume per week.
  • Intensity Zone Chart — Distribution of your training across intensity zones based on RPE.
  • Recovery Summary Card — Average sleep, stress, and recovery score trend.

Recommendations

New Personalization

The Recommendation Engine provides intelligent, data-driven suggestions to optimize your training. It works on two levels:

Rule-Based Recommendations

Six built-in rules analyze your data and generate actionable insights. The engine runs deduplication — it won't repeat the same active recommendation.

#RuleTriggerAction
1Low RecoveryAvg recovery score < 50 over 3 daysSuggests reducing volume 20-30%, prioritize sleep
2Stagnant VolumeVolume not increased in 3 weeks with good recoverySuggests adding sets or increasing weight
3Lift WeaknessBench/squat ratio < 65% or deadlift/squat < 110%Identifies weak lift, suggests accessory focus
4Competition PeakingCompetition goal within 28 daysSuggests peaking phase: reduce volume 40%, high intensity
5Goal NearGoal progress ≥ 90%Motivational message with remaining progress
6No Recovery DataNo recovery logs in 3 daysReminds user to log recovery for accurate recommendations

AI-Powered Analysis

Using OpenRouter (free-tier LLM: meta-llama/llama-3.1-8b-instruct), users can request a deep AI analysis of their training data.

  • Context window — 4-12 weeks of training data sent to the AI.
  • Data included — Experience level, estimated 1RMs, weekly volume, recovery trends, lift ratios, active goals, and current rule-based recommendations.
  • Rate limit — 1 request per 24 hours per user.
  • Fallback model — If the primary model is unavailable, it falls back to google/gemma-2-9b-it:free.

The AI returns 2-3 specific program recommendations for the upcoming week, tailored to your data.

Note: Requires OPENROUTER_API_KEY to be set in the environment. Without it, AI analysis returns a 502 error.

Notifications & Reminders

New Personalization

A comprehensive notification system keeps you on track with your training:

Notification Types

  • Reminder Recovery — Prompt to log today's recovery data.
  • Reminder Lift — Reminder to log your training session.
  • Goal Achieved — Alert when a goal is automatically detected as achieved.
  • Goal Near — Encouragement when a goal is close to completion.
  • Recommendation — Notification when new recommendations are generated.

Notification Preferences

Each user can configure their notification preferences:

  • Reminder Recovery — Toggle on/off, set reminder time (HH:MM), and days of week.
  • Reminder Lift — Toggle on/off, set reminder time, and days of week.
  • Telegram Bot — Connect Telegram for push notifications (requires TELEGRAM_BOT_TOKEN env var).

Scheduler Jobs

Three background jobs run in the backend (enabled via SCHEDULER_ENABLED=true):

JobScheduleFunction
Reminder JobEvery 60 minutesSends pending recovery and lift reminders based on user preferences and current time.
Goal CheckDaily at 00:05Automatically marks goals as achieved when current values meet targets.
CleanupDaily at 03:00Deletes expired recommendations and notifications older than 90 days.
